Possible solutions

  • Education

    Improving education particularly around healthcare related matters in Wales could improve poor health outcomes by allowing both adults and children to make more informed lifestyle choices and make better usage of the healthcare services that are available for them. Education plays a crucial role in understanding preventative measures, nutrition and healthy eating and mental health, equiping people to make healthier descisions. Additionally, this should allow more people to maintain meaningful employment as they take less time off being unwell meaning the government can recover more money in taxes to fund the NHS.

  • Increase Funding

    Increasing NHS funding in Wales could improve health outcomes by reducing wait times, increasing access to services and enhancing the quality of care. With more resources available, the NHS could expand staff numbers and invest in modern equipment and technology allowin for faster diagnosis and more effective treatments. Further investment in the NHS could provide more consistent and high quality care for Welsh communities ultimately leading to a healthier population and less strain on healthcare resources.

  • Recruit more doctors

    Recruiting more doctors in Wales could greatly improve health outcomes by addressing critical shortages, reducing wait times and ensuring patients recieve timely care. With high numbers of doctors, the NHS could manage demand more effectively allowing for shorter appointment wait times. More doctors could also reduce the stress felt by overworked existing staff which could reduce turnover rates, staff burnout and improve job satisfaction. All this leads to a higher quality of care.

  • Preventative care

    Expanding preventative care in Wales could significantly improve health outcomes by addressing health issues before they become servere or expensive to treat. Preventative care, including regular checks and vaccinations can help catch diseases early. It can also encourage a healthier lifestyle which lowers risks associated with smoking, poor diet and inactivity. By investing in preventative care measures the, Wales could reduce the burden on the NHS and improve overall health outcomes making healthcare more sustainable and accessible long term.
